My Process

I am going to show you how I use my Ipad to create some digital illustrations like these...

On this page, I'm going to share my process on how I create original pieces like this using procreate. These techniques are useful not just for the ipad, but they can be applied to any art software. Here are some of the specifications of what I will be working on:

  • IPad with Procreate installed
  • Include Brushes
  • 4000x2000 Canvas set to RGB (any size canvas is fine depending on your preference. In fact, for the sketching phase, I used an 18x24 inch canvas, then switched)
  • Imagination!!!!

    • Step One: Skecting

    The first step in my process is always the idea. This is the most challenging, but most fun portion of this process because this is where I can come up with ideas.

    For this piece, I wanted to create a simple scene of whales coming together. Using different brushes, I planned out where I wanted each whale to be and how I wanted to see them based on perspective and composition.

    This phase is the foundation to the rest of your painting. Without a good direction on where you want to take your art, it will be very difficult to define what else you want to see. So remember to take your time just create. Once your're satisfied with the placement, its time to move to step two.

    Step Two: Refined Drawing

    Now it is time to get into work. Now that you have your sketch, its time to tie it down with a more finished drawing. Using another layer, start drawing over your sketch until you've added the details you seem desireable.

    When I was tying down this painting, I consistently looked at the anatomy of my subjects and making sure any details I should add are added. This is especially important when your subject is a real, living thing. So if that's the case, reference is really helpful.

    On another note, making sure you take the time to include details is important. For the sake of time, I decided to leave it as it was.

    Step Three: Basic Color

    Now it's time to start adding color! What I like to do when I create my paintings, I focus on what I call "basic color". Basic color is the color you would make an object that doesn't have any light or shadow added to it.

    One great thing about using procreate is how veratile and robust the color tools are. You can choose your own colors or even pick colors from photographs by holding down with one finger. this creates an eyedropper function that can be used for color accuracy.

    Since my subjects are only two colors, I usually like to add some minor color variations to make them more alive. This helps to make your paintings less boring. I also included a basic background on a separate layer to establish my "atmosphere" (more on that later...)

    Step Four: Lighting and Shadow

    When using procreate, there are two layer modes I use when to make light and shadow. Those are Multiply and Overlay. Multiply takes the color you're using and "multiplies" it to create a dark effect. Overlay does the exact same thing, but instead, it settles on top-making it lighter instead.

    Using two separate layers for these effects, I usually try to focus on a specific direction. However, since our scene takes place in water, I'm going ot have to thinga about my light and shadow differently. That's were atmosphere starts to play a roll.

    When we look at a landscape, there is a reason that you can't see past a certain distance. The atmosphere is not invisible. It is an object we are looking through. No different from a window. When you look further and further ahead, you can see more layers of the atmosphere, hence why things become faded over long distances.

    And since we are painting in an aquatic scene, the effect is multiplied by 10.

    Taking this into account, I try to paint according to how light is reflected and refracted by all the subjects of my scene compared to my setting.

    I'd like to say that art is as much technical thinking as it is creative thinking.

    Step Five: Adding Details

    Finally, we can add some details. At this stage, its now time to render out the painting until i'm satisfied with how it looks.

    At this final stage, I try to focus on different aspects of the art like abient lighting, refractions, details on the whales, particle effects, and so much more. I won't go into too much depth over this process because this stage is all stylistic.

    This stage is also good to touch up any remaining details you mightv'e missed. Here you can be as thorough or as basic as you'd like. This is the stage where I make things pretty.

    There you have it. That's a basic introduction to how I create my art pieces. Now that you have the knowledge, try for yourself and see what you come up with. But remember, an artist is only as good as the will to create, not the tools he has. I just gave you some techniques so you can make your own art, so you can use them however way you'd like!

    Have fun creating! and as one of my favorite illustrator/animator Aaron Blaise says...

    "Put some beauty back into the world!"
